## 2026 Strategic Risks

### 1. Brand Recognition Gap
- LevelShift brand is still building after October 2025 rebrand
- PreludeSys had 27 years of brand equity in its target markets; LevelShift is effectively starting over on brand awareness
- **Mitigation:** Use PreludeSys heritage explicitly ("formerly PreludeSys, now LevelShift") in client conversations; lead with track record, not brand name

### 2. AI Commoditisation Risk
- Rapid tooling advancement (GPT-5, Claude 4+, Gemini) could compress margins on AI implementation work that becomes turnkey
- Firms that compete on tool access will be commoditised; LevelShift must compete on methodology, governance, and outcome accountability
- **Mitigation:** The 5-Phase methodology, 150+ pre-built agents, and case study ROI metrics are defensible differentiators; tooling access is not

### 3. Enterprise AI Proof Requirements
- Buyers increasingly require **production references**, not roadmaps or pilots
- The shift from "we plan to do this" to "we did this and here are the results" is accelerating
- **Mitigation:** Always lead with SPINX, CITGO, Ongweoweh ŌĆö never open a pitch without a named reference from the relevant vertical

### 4. Certified Talent Market Compression
- Salesforce Summit Partner and Microsoft Solutions Partner status requires maintaining certification thresholds
- Certified AI, Fabric, Agentforce, and D365 talent is in high demand across the industry
- **Mitigation:** Leverage Great Place to Work status and culture narrative in talent attraction; offshore delivery model in Chennai provides volume buffer

### 5. Single-Platform Competitors Narrowing the Gap
- Boutique Salesforce and Microsoft firms are beginning to add AI capabilities
- The cross-platform advantage is durable but not permanent
- **Mitigation:** Accelerate the integration story ŌĆö clients who run both Salesforce and Microsoft need a single partner, not two boutiques
