## 9. Output Instructions for LLM

When generating the `levelshiftAngle` field for any news item, follow these rules:

1. **Be specific.** Reference named LevelShift capabilities, services, case studies, partnerships, or certifications. Never write "this is relevant to LevelShift's AI work" — say *which* work, *why*, and *what the team should do*.

2. **Include a call to action.** Every angle should end with something actionable: a sales talking point, a workshop angle, a client outreach idea, or a messaging update.

3. **Use the correct lens.** Match the news item to one or more of the 7 lenses above. A Microsoft Fabric update always uses Lens 2. An AI governance story uses Lens 4. A failure story uses Lens 5. Some stories use multiple lenses.

4. **Reference real LevelShift assets where possible:**
   - Case studies: SPINX, CITGO, Ongweoweh, New York Blood Center, NatureBridge, Lereta LLC, UnitedAg, Ever.Ag
   - Frameworks: 5-Phase Value Realization Framework, LDMF (Data Modernisation Framework), CLAPQ
   - Credentials: Salesforce Summit Partner, Agentforce Ready, 25+ Fabric engineers, 150+ pre-built agents, SOC 2, CMMI
   - Verticals: Manufacturing, Financial Services, Healthcare (primary); Retail, Technology, Energy (secondary)

5. **Relevance score guidance (1–10):**
   - 9–10: Directly affects a LevelShift service line, active client, or partner relationship (e.g. Salesforce Agentforce update, Microsoft Fabric release, EU AI Act deadline)
   - 7–8: Strongly relevant to a target vertical or sales conversation (e.g. AI ROI benchmark for FinServ, manufacturing AI adoption data)
   - 5–6: Useful background context for the team (e.g. general AI productivity research, emerging tools)
   - 3–4: Tangentially relevant — include only if no stronger story available
   - 1–2: Do not include

6. **Avoid generic statements** like:
   - "This is relevant to LevelShift as an AI company"
   - "LevelShift should monitor this development"
   - "This highlights the importance of AI governance"
   - "LevelShift clients may be affected by this"

   Instead write:
   - "Our 25+ Fabric-certified engineers and the SPINX case study position us to lead this conversation with retail clients immediately"
   - "The 5-Phase methodology addresses the exact failure mode described — use this as a battle card in pipeline conversations with Manufacturing prospects"
   - "Our SOC 2 certification and governance playbooks are the proof points EU/UK clients will ask for before the August deadline"
